Patriots Sign Stefon Diggs: What This Means For His Fantasy Stock

The New England Patriots recently signed wide receiver Stefon Diggs to a three-year deal worth up to $69 million, with $26 million guaranteed. The veteran receiver will likely serve as Drake Maye’s WR1 during the 2025 season, and he should see a flurry of targets from the promising young quarterback.

Diggs was having a productive season with the Houston Texans in 2024, recording 47 receptions for 496 yards and three touchdowns, before tearing his ACL in a week eight matchup against the Indianapolis Colts. It will be tough for the 31-year-old to return to pro-bowl form, but he gave Patriots fans some hope when he said he is ahead of schedule on his recovery during a recent press conference.

The signing puts Diggs’ fantasy stock in an awkward place. His career body of work screams WR1, but the uncertainty of his new situation and his recent injury history warrants a cautious approach. No one knows what this revamped Patriots offense is going to look like come September. They are still candidates to add another receiver in the draft, free agency, or via the trade market, further clouding Diggs’ fantasy outlook.

If Diggs is able to recover and the Patriots miss out on adding another weapon, he will be the primary focus of the passing offense and should see several targets per game, giving him high-end WR2 upside. If the Patriots sign another legitimate receiving threat, Diggs will receive less defensive attention and can play the same role for Maye that he did for second-year Texans QB C.J. Stroud – a reliable target and safety valve.

Diggs averaged 12.3 fantasy points per game in half-PPR scoring in 2024 before his injury, ranking 19th amongst all receivers, according to Sleeper. It is easy to imagine him having a similar season this year with the Patriots, if he can stay healthy.

However, it is still uncertain whether Diggs will be ready for week one, and there is always the possibility that he does not have the same elite route-running abilities that he relies on to get open.

Also, the Patriots offense was among the league’s worst last season, ranking second to last in yards per game, according to ESPN. New head coach Mike Vrabel and returning offensive coordinator Josh McDaniels should bring some stability to the offense, but it would take drastic improvement for the offense as a whole to be taken as a serious threat.

Diggs is likely to end up lost in the abyss of WR3s if his recovery isn’t smooth, posting inconsistent numbers and only really helping fantasy teams when he scores.

With all of the clutter surrounding his situation, Diggs is expected to be pushed down draft boards. He fits in perfectly as the third or fourth receiver on a fantasy roster, likely not a difference maker, but has upside to be an every-week starter.
